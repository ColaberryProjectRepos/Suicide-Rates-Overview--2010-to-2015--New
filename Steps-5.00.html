Model Performance


  1. R2 (Coefficient of Determination): 1.0
    • Interpretation: R2 measures the proportion of the variance in the dependent variable that is predictable from the independent variables. A score of 1.0 indicates a perfect fit, meaning the model explains all the variability in the target variable. While this might suggest an excellent fit, it's crucial to check for overfitting and validate on an independent dataset.
  2. Mean Squared Error: 3.9
    • Interpretation: Mean Squared Error (MSE) quantifies the average squared difference between predicted values and actual values. A lower MSE is generally desirable, and 3.9 suggests relatively low error on average. However, the interpretation can depend on the scale of your target variable.
  3. Mean Absolute Error: 1.1
    • Interpretation: Mean Absolute Error (MAE) represents the average absolute difference between predicted and actual values. A lower MAE indicates better model performance. In this case, 1.1 suggests relatively small errors on average.
  4. Median Absolute Error: 0.6
    • Interpretation: Similar to MAE, Median Absolute Error provides a measure of the central tendency of prediction errors. A lower value, such as 0.6, indicates that half of the absolute errors are below this value, showcasing good model accuracy.
  5. Variance: 1.0
    • Interpretation: Variance measures the spread or dispersion of predicted values from the mean. A variance of 1.0 suggests that predictions are perfectly aligned with the actual values, indicating a precise model.
