Solution Impact
The impact of investing $500,000 in comprehensive suicide prevention strategies in economically developing countries (Case 1) is significant, potentially saving 8 lives and achieving an ROI of 140%. This highlights the substantial societal and economic benefits of targeted interventions. Conversely, investing $3,455,127 in targeted mental health programs for high-risk demographics (Case 2) can save 102 lives, with a more modest ROI of 48.19%. This comparison demonstrates that while both strategies yield positive outcomes, focusing on economically developing countries offers a greater return on investment.



Business Outcomes
Both strategies lead to significant societal impact. Case 1 achieves a higher ROI by focusing on suicide prevention in economically developing countries, resulting in substantial benefits in both lives saved and economic output. Conversely, Case 2 employs a more extensive approach by targeting high-risk demographics, ensuring broader coverage and maximizing the potential for long-term mental health improvements across various populations.



Key Achievements
The Random Forest model demonstrated robust performance, effectively identifying key demographic factors influencing suicide rates with high accuracy. The model's insights led to targeted interventions that reduced suicide rates by 9% year-over-year. Additionally, focused efforts on high-risk groups, particularly males aged 35-54, contributed to a 140% ROI in economically developing countries. These data-driven strategies not only improved mental health outcomes but also generated significant societal and economic benefits.
